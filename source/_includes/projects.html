Lessoneer

An online curriculum management software platform called Lessoneer that is used by K- 12 school districts to build, access, and share math, language arts, and science curricula.

Helping 1000's of teachers every day, Lessoner accomplishes this using Ruby on Rails for a backend, and React for our complex front-end interactions. Using CircleCI for Continous Integration, and practicing Contious Delivery on our AWS infrastructure. We deliver a constantly improving system, to help our teachres save time.

Resourcify

There are a number of freely avilable curriculum that teachers use. However, these curriculum are hosted in a few different places across the web. There are some great lists of supplemental resources, but they aren't available on any of the sites.

This is where Resourcify steps in. We built a content-aware Chrome Extension, that asks a lightning fast Ruby on Rails API if there are any known resources for the page the teacher is currently looking at. With over 2,000 active users Resroucify helps teachres find the resrouces they need.

Splitr

Often in the education space, freely available curriculum can be found in PDF format. However, these PDFs can be hundreds of pages long, with the teacher needing to print, or digitally hand out a single page. While there are utilities for breaking up a PDF, we wanted one that was education focused, and could suggest pages for things like "Chapter 7 Homework"

Utilizing PDFtk to manipulate PDFs, Splitr uses React, Ruby on Rails, and PDF.js to help teachres quickly find the documents they need. With a database of documents-within-documents available to them, Splitr helps teachres find the document they need, and even allow them to share it to Google Classroom.

Classroomify

Classroomify was born out of an internal requirement. We had added Google's own Add to Google Classroom button to our product. But our user's weren't happy with the way Google had implemented it. User's wanted a way to create an assignment, share it to their class, or

Classroomify was built to not only fill the gaps in Google's Add to Classroom button, but it was also built with other developers in mind. Believing that we weren't the only Ed-Tech startup interested in this fucntionality, Classroomify was architected in a way that would allow it to be offered as a SaaS product.
